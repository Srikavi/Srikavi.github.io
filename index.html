When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$

The Right Triangle

Base Geometry

![](./triangle.png)

Let the right triangle hypothenuse be aligned with the coordinate system x-axis. The vector loop closure equation running counter-clockwise then reads

$$a\boldsymbol{e}_\alpha + b\tilde\boldsymbol{e}_\alpha + c\boldsymbol{e}_x = \boldsymbol{0}$$ (1)

with

$$\boldsymbol{e}_\alpha = \begin{pmatrix}\cos\alpha\\ \sin\alpha\end{pmatrix} \quad and \quad {\tilde\boldsymbol{e}}_\alpha = \begin{pmatrix}-\sin\alpha\\ \cos\alpha\end{pmatrix}$$

Resolving for the hypothenuse part $c{\bold e}_x$ in the loop closure equation (1)

$$-c\boldsymbol{e}_x = a{\boldsymbol{e}}_\alpha + b\tilde{\boldsymbol{e}}_\alpha$$

and squaring

finally results in the Pythagorean theorem (2) $$c^2 = a^2 + b^2$$ (2)
